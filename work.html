Somebody - AE 3D Credits

3D environments and animation - After Effects

For this project, a 3D environment in After Effects was requested. The goal was to create the start or ending credits of a film or show using the 3D environment tools from After Effects. Other directions as for duration, techniques and resolution were given.

Once understood the briefing, I took the approach that best fit my content of choice. In this case, a recently watched series from Netflix called Somebody. The show is in korean, thus the names of the actors are displayed using hangeul, the korean alphabet and some of the content as well combined with English.

First, I started gathering reference images, as many snaps from the show to grasp as best as I could the colorimetry of the show, the style and review the important and key elements of the show. This helped me later on define the color scheme and graphics.

I also gather the data from the production of the show and main actors to have the proper data to work with. Then I searched for other examples and references from other credit animations to inspire my work. With all of this in mind, I was capable of slowly picturing the kind of storyline I wanted and the type of 3D environment I wanted to use.

Now I was capable of making a story board with the frames, events, elements and key animations and transition that I wanted to create. And like so the graphics, typography, sound design, colors and image styles were also defined.

Fractured - Movie Trailer

Video Editing and Narrative - Adobe Premiere & After Effects

The briefing given set that a trailer on a movie or show had to be done with a duration of 45 seconds. Also, details as for resolution, subtitling, transitions, audio mix and other techniques to be included were given. The main project was to be done via usage of Premiere Pro with certain text animation add-ons from After Effects.

Taking the details into account, I decided to base my trailer on a recently watched Brad Anderson film called Fractured. As a thriller it has nice tension moments that I felt excited to work around. I begin the project watching the film again while creating subclips from the most interesting points from the film that I felt I could include in the trailer.

Once I had these high tension, detailed in info or nice visual clips I started building the idea of the narrative that I wanted to show in the trailer. And by building and changing this blocks of info, finding a balance that would create enough interest and hype in the spectator. After the clips and narrative was set, animations, transitions, subtitles etc. were added on to the project to better translate information and feeling.

Moon TV - TV Autopromo spot

Video Editing and Branding - Adobe Premiere & After Effects

The challenge for this project was to create an autopromo spot for a streaming platform also to be designed by us branding and content style wise. Certain elements like intro and outro, a bug or DOG, and certain text animations. Plus the requested time duration and resolution of the spot.

The channel content and design was an open brief. Thus, I decided to create one for k-dramas, as I often consume this content. I started creating a list of dramas I had watched and classifying them into categories to better see how I could make an approach to the channel. To give it a style and direction.

This way I saw that despite being amazing dark thrillers and horror dramas, the vast majority of the content is based on romance, comedy and melodramas. This not only gave me a great overview of how to take over the spot but also gave me a direction to follow for the graphic design.

I decided to make a romantic, cute and fun design, as most of the content of the channel would match this direction. Therefore, light, cute pastel colors, a fun type and fun elements could be added. I decided to name and base my logo around the moon.

In Asian culture, the moon is often related to romance and love stories. It’s not only romance but also a mysterious figure, beautiful but also distant and unknown. This deeper meanings behind the moon covered the darker side of k-dramas that would be otherwise be missing in such a cutesy design. This way I found a way of crating balance and certain mystery and appeal.

Miin Cosmetics - Animation Spot

Text and video animation Spot - After Effects

In this case, an advertisement spot was requested that had to includes kinetic typography effect along with a logo reveal and other animations via the use of of After Effects. So I decided to make a cosmetics add based on the already existing brand Miin Cosmetics. This brand is quite popular in Spain for selling k-beauty products.

I dived into Miin Cosmetics’ style and design elements to recreate them and use them in my animation spot. This way I had graphics and a base of elements I could work around. It also helped me familiarize myself with their tone, priorities and products and services that are provided by the brand.

With this in mind I build a story line, a script for the Kinetic type section and was capable of starting to create and design the animations as I worked on it. Also, to do the logo reveal I wanted to use the hangeul or korean alphabet element in it, so I came up with a famous and cohesive tag line to blend into the logo.

This tag line is “완전 촉촉해!” that comes to say “It’s so moisturizing!”, quite suitable for a cosmetics brand. As the “ㅊ” is part of the logo and the sentence as well, it was the perfect animation key.

Pastries Heaven - Chanel Intro Animation

For this project a fake stop-motion channel intro was requested. A certain range of duration and resolution was also given. Being free to choose the kind of channel to make an intro for, I decided to create the intro for a pastries channel.

Vivienne Westwood Lighter - 3D

3D Modeling and Animation - Cinema 4D

A spot 3D animated commercial or brand video art was requested. Both modelling and animation. I then began to research products that I could model, animate and adore. It all came around to this Vivienne Westwood lighter that I’m obsessed with. The punkiness and girliness to it was a really attractive balance. As a fan of Nana it also made sense.


I first started gathering inspiration from Nana comics, references, textures and just overall graphic tendencies around the brand and the surrounding fandom. I decided to direct my content towards social media and digital platforms, giving it a vertical format to better fit smartphones. I also pieced out and thought of the mechanism and how to make it work for the modelling process and animation.

Like so, I looked for texture, modelling and lighting inspiration. Created mood-boards to inspire and help me conform what elements and shapes could be added to the project. Also looked up for the clearest references I could find to properly model the shape of the lighter, as it’s not just round or square and has quite distinctive curves.


All of this work guided me to then build a story board for the main animations I wanted, and also experiment a little to bring effects and create an interesting story line that would guide the video and animations. I first modelled the lighter in Cinema 4D. Once the two pieces of the lighter were build and then set together properly for the right turning on movement, the lighter was ready to animate.


I built the different sequences of the separately and then the project was put together, and final touches added in After Effects.

Eternal - The peak of Teas

Branding, Illustration, Product and Packaging design - Procreate, Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop

This project is based on a modern reinterpretation of Kafka's work “Metamorphosis”. This reinterpretation puts the focus on how the presence of technology has changed our lives, and how it transforms the very concepts addressed in Kafka’s “The Metamorphosis”. First, I present my work as for how I saw and understood the concept, through my style and the tech gadgets and material prepared for the shooting and concept. Then this was also integrated in a group project where it is again transformed manually to become part of a magazine and work on different concepts inside the novel.

Eternal became the perfect name for this brand, being teas that age with time and become better and better, this name, for an always alive product, made sense. This type of tea also has it’s origin on the Ancient 14 Mountains of Tea in the province of Yunnan, in Pu’erh area that also gives name to this tea variety. The origin of tea, implies pu’erh and fermented teas. Teas both everlasting and complex in flavour. That’s why I chose to refer to the brand Eternal as “The Peak of Teas”.


Following the concept of eternal, I chose the circle, a symbol of endlessness and also eternity, as the core element of the illustrations for my teas. Like so, I created a brush on Procreate with a circle as a base that allowed me to create diverse illustrations and motives for the teas and brand.

Maiko no Kaori - Speciality Beer Design

Branding, Illustration, Product and Packaging design - Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop

Maiko no Kaori is a beer packaging project that I hold a lot of sweet and warm feelings for. It is precious and unique. I took inspiration from the japanese series “Makanai maiko chef” in japanese “舞妓さんちのまかないさん”. A beer is something refreshing, and thinking of that feeling I though of the freshness and youth both in japanese spring and in maiko. Both new beginnings. With this concept of freshness and something new, I wanted to provide flavours that would be new, refreshing and also remind us of spring.

That’s how I came up with the flavours of sakura or cherry blossoms, matcha, and mikan the special blended variety between orange and tangerine from Japan. This flavours match perfectly my concept. I also wanted to represent something unique from maiko in my packaging.


After learning through the series and my own research, I decided to make the characteristic maiko lip the center of the concept in the packaging. Maikos only paint one lip while being maiko, and only when becoming Geiko or as more commonly known, Geisha, both lips are painted when performing.


After having both flavours and concept I begun to design. I wanted something lightweight, fun, naive and cute. While keeping that fresh tone. That’s why I chose light and bright colors to transmit that spring palette. Also chose thinner types, and an overall cleaner look while keeping it fun.

MetaMorfosis - Photography & Art Direction

Photography and Art Direction - Photoshop, Crafting, Design

This project is based on a modern reinterpretation of Kafka's work “Metamorphosis”. This reinterpretation puts the focus on how the presence of technology has changed our lives, and how it transforms the very concepts addressed in Kafka’s “The Metamorphosis”. First, I present my work as for how I saw and understood the concept, through my style and the tech gadgets and material prepared for the shooting and concept. Then this was also integrated in a group project where it is again transformed manually to become part of a magazine and work on different concepts inside the novel.

A vision delving into the META-morphosis. Since this label “meta” is used for technology, AI, and developments within the digital/technological realm, it sounded great. It translates a new meaning to Metamorphosis as pure innovation, change, and technology. It becomes a space to reflect on how technology blurs the boundary between human and machine. In a society that values only productivity, failing to contribute risks turning us into “monsters”—dispensable, disruptive beings. This utilitarian worldview distorts both social reality and our own self-perception, raising the question: what truly separates us from machines?

This photographic reinterpretation combines analog and digital techniques to explore Kafka’s Metamorphosis. By blending technology with analog manipulation, I seek both a symbolic depth and a tactile, emotional connection to the novel. The deliberate imperfections of analog processes contrast with digital precision, creating images that highlight the duality between the tangible and the abstract, while reflecting the psychological and emotional dimensions of Gregor Samsa’s transformation.

Aire - Editorial Fashion Photography

Photography, Production and Art Direction - Capture One, Photoshop

Aire is a fashion photography project that aims to capture the essence of freedom, elegance and braveness in a delicate way. To begin and guide the project, a concept moodboard was created. Once the view was set, other moodboards were created to guide the type of poses, the light, the fashion, the make-up, composition etc. Setting these guidelines helped not only production, and knowing what and how to photograph, but also guided the model to better know how to perform.

After setting up the concept, pre-production begun which involved, preparing make-up bags, buying clothes, confirming and arranging dates with the team and of course looking for an adequate location. When the time comes, everything is ready, and you just hope for people to be on time. Which thankfully happened. And you shall always remember to charge your camera batteries. Luckily, I did :). And not to forget to carefully not brake tags on the clothes to try and get your money back.

After shooting, selection phase begins. That means going through the billion pictures you took just in case and choosing the ones that fit the guidelines best and that aren’t out of focus hehe. After selection, editing out any imperfections and at last color correction. This project was quite stressful for many reasons, but I loved shooting it and felt very rewarded with the results.
