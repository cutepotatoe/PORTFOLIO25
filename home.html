Vivienne Westwood Lighter - 3D

3D Modeling and Animation - Cinema 4D

A spot 3D animated commercial or brand video art was requested. Both modelling and animation. I then began to research products that I could model, animate and adore. It all came around to this Vivienne Westwood lighter that I’m obsessed with. The punkiness and girliness to it was a really attractive balance. As a fan of Nana it also made sense.


I first started gathering inspiration from Nana comics, references, textures and just overall graphic tendencies around the brand and the surrounding fandom. I decided to direct my content towards social media and digital platforms, giving it a vertical format to better fit smartphones. I also pieced out and thought of the mechanism and how to make it work for the modelling process and animation.

Like so, I looked for texture, modelling and lighting inspiration. Created mood-boards to inspire and help me conform what elements and shapes could be added to the project. Also looked up for the clearest references I could find to properly model the shape of the lighter, as it’s not just round or square and has quite distinctive curves.


All of this work guided me to then build a story board for the main animations I wanted, and also experiment a little to bring effects and create an interesting story line that would guide the video and animations. I first modelled the lighter in Cinema 4D. Once the two pieces of the lighter were build and then set together properly for the right turning on movement, the lighter was ready to animate.


I built the different sequences of the separately and then the project was put together, and final touches added in After Effects.

Somebody - AE 3D Credits

3D environments and animation - After Effects

For this project, a 3D environment in After Effects was requested. The goal was to create the start or ending credits of a film or show using the 3D environment tools from After Effects. Other directions as for duration, techniques and resolution were given.

Once understood the briefing, I took the approach that best fit my content of choice. In this case, a recently watched series from Netflix called Somebody. The show is in korean, thus the names of the actors are displayed using hangeul, the korean alphabet and some of the content as well combined with English.

First, I started gathering reference images, as many snaps from the show to grasp as best as I could the colorimetry of the show, the style and review the important and key elements of the show. This helped me later on define the color scheme and graphics.

I also gather the data from the production of the show and main actors to have the proper data to work with. Then I searched for other examples and references from other credit animations to inspire my work. With all of this in mind, I was capable of slowly picturing the kind of storyline I wanted and the type of 3D environment I wanted to use.

Now I was capable of making a story board with the frames, events, elements and key animations and transition that I wanted to create. And like so the graphics, typography, sound design, colors and image styles were also defined.

Miso - Magazine Design & Production

Branding, Content Design and Photography - InDesign, Ps, Ai and Capture One

Miso is a Magazine that is born as a group project between some uni colleagues inspired by Asian culture and geek or otaku niches. The goal was to create the concept, design and production of a magazine by ourselves. We aimed to do so in a fun and authentic way led by our interests and inspiration, and in a way that we could put our skills in to action.


We started defining the theme of the magazine, the type of content, the frequency of publishment and style. Then we structured the length, content and times of production. This way, we arranged an overview for the project to better guide ourselves and distribute tasks.


All of our images and content was self-made, all photographs were taken personally, illustrations were also by the team, and the interviews real and then transcribed. We tried to make this experience as real, and authentic as possible. Being informative, while fun, and setting a tone of reference and inspiration for the community.

Eternal - The peak of Teas

Branding, Illustration, Product and Packaging design - Procreate, Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop

This project is based on a modern reinterpretation of Kafka's work “Metamorphosis”. This reinterpretation puts the focus on how the presence of technology has changed our lives, and how it transforms the very concepts addressed in Kafka’s “The Metamorphosis”. First, I present my work as for how I saw and understood the concept, through my style and the tech gadgets and material prepared for the shooting and concept. Then this was also integrated in a group project where it is again transformed manually to become part of a magazine and work on different concepts inside the novel.

Eternal became the perfect name for this brand, being teas that age with time and become better and better, this name, for an always alive product, made sense. This type of tea also has it’s origin on the Ancient 14 Mountains of Tea in the province of Yunnan, in Pu’erh area that also gives name to this tea variety. The origin of tea, implies pu’erh and fermented teas. Teas both everlasting and complex in flavour. That’s why I chose to refer to the brand Eternal as “The Peak of Teas”.


Following the concept of eternal, I chose the circle, a symbol of endlessness and also eternity, as the core element of the illustrations for my teas. Like so, I created a brush on Procreate with a circle as a base that allowed me to create diverse illustrations and motives for the teas and brand.

Natural Intelligence - Case Study

Advertisement - Team Proyect

Team project - Awarded: Merit, Young Ones Show (The One Club) 2024


Case Study proposal for Young Ones One Show on Dove. This proposal pin points the the side effects of using artificial intelligence in advertising is the exacerbation of stereotypes. This is especially harmful to self-esteem and self-perception, particularly for younger generations. To help address this issue, a mock ad was designed for Dove in collaboration with OpenAI, where DALL·E is trained to generate beauty images that are not solely based on imposed standards.

Illusion of Life - Animation of a still Illustration

Segmentation, Editing, Animation - Photoshop, AE

This challenge was to bring an illustration to life, and so I chose this one from Nanjiao Xi. First, I thought about the key movements I wanted to create. After organizing the plan, The illustration was cut up in the necessary pieces and reconstructed where it was necessary to make it as seamless as possible. After that, through After Effects, I was able to bring the Illustration to life. I wanted to maintain the sophisticate touch of the woman and transmit so elegantly to the movement. But I wanted to also spice it up a bit thorough the light and playful smirk added at the end of the animation.

Johnnie Walker - Product Photography

Photography, Production and Art Direction - Capture One, Photoshop

For this photography project, we had to choose a product to photograph and prepare the set for the theme of such product. In this case, as a whisky was chosen, elements that would share environment with it appear. Vinyls, cards, games, classic representation of a whisky on the rocks etc. Once the mood board was set and the necessary elements decided, they were acquired and prepared for the shooting day in studio. After the shooting, pictures were edited and retouched to erase unnecessary wrinkles or shines.
